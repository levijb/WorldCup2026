## Today's Matches

**Canada vs. Morocco — 1:00 PM ET | NRG Stadium, Houston, Texas**
Canada's first-ever Round of 16 appearance runs straight into a Morocco side unbeaten in 33 games and fresh off eliminating the Netherlands — the co-hosts' toughest test by a wide margin. Canada finished second in Group B (draw Bosnia, a 6-0 rout of Qatar with a Jonathan David hat-trick, a loss to Switzerland) before Stephen Eustaquio's 92nd-minute winner beat South Africa for the program's first-ever World Cup knockout win; Morocco finished second behind Brazil in Group C, then needed penalties to get past a Netherlands side that controlled large stretches of their Round of 32 tie. Ismael Kone is out for the rest of the tournament with a broken leg, but Alphonso Davies is set for his first start after being limited to a substitute cameo last time out, while Morocco reports a fully fit squad with Chadi Riad recovered from a fitness scare. NRG Stadium's retractable roof will be closed, keeping conditions climate-controlled regardless of the mid-90s heat outside. Canada's underlying shot data — more shots on target than any side left in the tournament — argues this is a tighter match than a -125 Morocco price fully reflects.

**Paraguay vs. France — 5:00 PM ET | Lincoln Financial Field, Philadelphia, Pennsylvania**
France have scored three or more goals in every single match this tournament; Paraguay are averaging 0.36 expected goals and needed one of the great shootout performances just to reach this stage — this is close to the widest talent gap of the round. France won Group I perfectly before routing Sweden 3-0 in the Round of 32 (a Mbappe brace), while Paraguay scraped through Group D as a third-place qualifier (a 4-1 opening loss to the USA, a man-down win over Turkiye, a scoreless draw with Australia) before Julio Enciso's goal and Orlando Gill's shootout heroics eliminated Germany. Marcus Thuram (calf) remains a doubt for France but wouldn't start regardless given the form of Dembele, Olise, and Barcola ahead of him; Enciso and Gabriel Avalos are both listed as game-time decisions for Paraguay but are expected to start. Philadelphia forecasts call for mid-90s heat at kickoff, a real factor at an open-air venue for the less conditioned, deeper squad. The question tonight isn't whether France advances — it's whether Gill can conjure another shootout-caliber performance against a finishing quality several tiers above what beat Germany.

<a name="predictions"></a>
## Predictions

#### Canada vs. Morocco
Canada's underlying attacking numbers are the most encouraging story of their tournament — more shots on target than any other side left in the draw — but they've managed just two goals across their last two matches, an efficiency gap that becomes existential against a Morocco side that's reached this stage by finding a way rather than dominating (five shots on goal total in going through on penalties against the Netherlands). Jonathan David's 3.05 expected goals ranks sixth among every player left in the tournament, yet he's collected only three actual goals — the kind of gap that tends to close eventually. Morocco's spine of Hakimi, Bouaddi, and Saibari gives them control in midfield that Canada hasn't faced from an African side this tournament, but Alphonso Davies making his first start adds a direct, one-on-one puzzle Morocco haven't had to solve yet.

If Canada can keep this level into the final 20 minutes, their set-piece height and home energy in Houston give them a puncher's chance, but Morocco's knockout pedigree from their 2022 semifinal run is exactly the experience gap that tends to matter when games open up late. Expect Morocco's control to eventually find a breakthrough, with Canada's finishing quality determining whether this stays single-goal tight into extra time. Predicted score: 2–1 (Morocco).

#### Paraguay vs. France
France have scored three or more goals in every match this tournament, a level of attacking output that makes Paraguay's defensive resilience against Germany a different proposition entirely — Germany created 1.57 expected goals worth of chances and missed; France's front four convert at a rate that doesn't leave the same margin for error. Paraguay's approach hasn't changed and won't tonight: a compact, double-banked shape that cedes possession and hopes Orlando Gill produces another tournament-defining performance, but Gill was facing a German side that dominated territory without finishing, not one averaging 8.5 shots on target per match like France. Julio Enciso remains Paraguay's only individual source of a moment, the same read that applied against Germany, except this time there's no obvious defensive breakdown on the other side to exploit.

For Paraguay to have any hope, they need an early goal that forces France to actually break down a set defense rather than simply take the space they're given — a genuinely different challenge than anything France has faced this tournament. Barring that, this has the shape of a comfortable French win building steadily rather than an early rout. Predicted score: 3–0 (France).

## Around the Tournament

- Round of 16 begins on Independence Day — the winners of today's two matches meet first among the quarterfinalists, July 9 in Foxborough, Massachusetts.
- Mohamed Salah's Panenka in the shootout against Australia became instant World Cup history — FIFA's own site ran a feature tracing the penalty style's lineage at the tournament within hours of the final whistle.
- Colombia and Ghana were both forced into injury substitutions before the 15th minute of their match Friday — the first time that's happened between two teams in the same World Cup match on record.
- Cape Verde's debut tournament run ends as the story of the round: twice they leveled the score against the defending champions before Argentina finally escaped in extra time, cementing their place as the smallest nation by population ever to reach a men's World Cup knockout round.
- Messi's extra-time goal — his eighth straight World Cup appearance with at least one — pushed him to 7 for the tournament and outright ahead of Mbappe's 6 in the Golden Boot race, with Mbappe looking to answer back this afternoon against Paraguay.
