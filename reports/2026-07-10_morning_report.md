## Today's Match

<a name="spain-vs-belgium"></a>
**Spain vs. Belgium — 3:00 PM ET | SoFi Stadium, Inglewood, California**
Spain haven't conceded in six matches and Belgium is missing the midfielder built to disrupt exactly that kind of defensive structure — Amadou Onana tore his ACL against the USA and is out for the rest of the tournament, thinning the shield in front of a makeshift Belgium center-back pairing. Spain reached this stage the hard way, needing a 91st-minute substitute goal to get past Portugal, while Belgium arrives on the back of the tournament's most emphatic knockout win, a 4-1 demolition of the USA with Kevin De Bruyne deliberately rested. De Bruyne returns today, giving Rudi Garcia's attack its most dangerous version yet even without Onana shielding it. Both squads are otherwise healthy, the dome removes any weather variable, and the matchup sets up as the most genuinely contested quarterfinal of the round — Spain's suffocating structure against the first Belgium attack with the firepower to actually test it.

<a name="predictions"></a>
## Predictions

<a name="predictions-spain-vs-belgium"></a>
#### Spain vs. Belgium
Spain's defense is built on control rather than individual brilliance — Rodri screens more space than almost any midfielder left in the tournament, and the back four of Cucurella, Cubarsi, Laporte, and Porro hasn't been beaten once in five matches. Belgium's route through that has always run through Onana disrupting service into the final third; without him, Youri Tielemans and a reshaped midfield have to do that job alone, while a makeshift center-back pairing at the other end gives Yamal and Baena real space to work against on the flanks. Belgium's own case rests entirely on individual quality — De Bruyne's return restores a passer capable of finding gaps Spain hasn't had to defend yet, and their front line has scored freely all tournament, including four past a USA side that had real defensive spells of its own.

The most likely version of this match is Spain controlling territory and eventually breaking through via Oyarzabal or a wide combination, while Belgium's individual quality gives them enough to threaten without necessarily creating the sustained pressure needed to break a defense this well-organized. Spain's one knockout test so far came down to a stoppage-time goal, so a nervy finish or extra time are both live outcomes if Belgium's back line holds an early foothold. Predicted score: 2–1 (Spain).

## Around the Tournament

- Mbappe and Morocco captain Achraf Hakimi are close friends from their PSG days. Mbappe said there were "no emotions" on the pitch, but admitted seeing Hakimi afterward would hit him given how close they are off it.
- Mbappe's goal made him the first French player to reach 100 career goal involvements, doing it in just 104 appearances.
- Didier Deschamps reached his third consecutive World Cup semifinal as France manager — a run he'll close out this summer regardless of result, having already confirmed this is his last tournament in charge.
- France's win means they're guaranteed at least two more matches on American soil no matter what happens in the final rounds — the semifinal is already secured for July 14 in Arlington.
- Morocco's Bilal El Khannouss and Ayyoub Bouaddi (the latter just 18) both started the quarterfinal — Bouaddi became the youngest player in a World Cup quarterfinal since Pele in 1958, a marker of how much young talent Morocco has built around its senior core.
