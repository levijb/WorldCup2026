<a name="todays-match"></a>
## Today's Match

<a name="france-vs-morocco"></a>
**France vs. Morocco — 4:00 PM ET | Gillette Stadium, Foxborough, Massachusetts**
France have conceded twice in five matches and Mbappe has scored in four of five, but Morocco is missing the one player built to punish exactly this profile of France defense — Ismael Saibari, their leading scorer, is out with a hamstring injury, forcing a more cautious Morocco setup built around Soufiane Rahimi and Brahim Diaz. This is a rematch of the 2022 semifinal that France won 2-0, and Morocco has since become the first African nation to reach consecutive World Cup quarterfinals, unbeaten in 34 matches and matching the African-team scoring record for a single tournament. Tchouameni remains unlikely to start for France (thigh), keeping Manu Kone in central midfield, while Morocco gets center-back Chadi Riad back after he missed the Canada win. An all-Argentine officiating crew — a first for this tournament — has added a subplot neither manager wants to engage with publicly, but the football matchup itself favors the side with more attacking depth and a healthier spine.

- Chadi Riad (Morocco): Recovered from a knee issue, expected to start at center back over Redouane Halhal.

<a name="predictions"></a>
## Predictions

<a name="predictions-france-vs-morocco"></a>
#### France vs. Morocco
France's defensive record is the foundation of this match — two goals conceded in five games, and that was built without Tchouameni for large stretches of the knockout rounds. Morocco's attack loses its sharpest individual weapon in Saibari, who scored in every group match and converted the winning penalty against the Netherlands; Rahimi has been productive off the bench but hasn't shown the same end product as a starter, and Morocco's more realistic route to a chance now runs through set pieces and Ounahi's midfield bursts rather than sustained pressure. Mbappe has scored in four of five matches and draws the platform to keep doing so against a Morocco defense that had to work hard just to shut out Canada's limited attack.

Morocco's path to survival is a low-event match where Achraf Hakimi's experience and the back line's organization keep things level long enough to make it a contest, similar to how they ground down the Netherlands in the Round of 32. But France has more individual quality up front than anything Morocco has faced yet in the knockout rounds, and a below-full-strength Morocco attack gives Les Bleus more room to control tempo than the tight two-goal margins France has needed in its last two matches. Predicted score: 2–0 (France).

<a name="around-the-tournament"></a>
## Around the Tournament

- Argentina's comeback against Egypt is already being called one of the great World Cup games. Messi was in tears afterward; teammate Julian Alvarez called what he's doing "incredible," adding that the squad is just trying to support him and enjoy the ride.
- Egypt coach Hossam Hassan called the defeat an "injustice" and pointed to a VAR-disallowed goal and a waved-off penalty appeal as the reason his side lost a 2-0 lead. Independent review of both calls has largely backed the officials, and neutral analysis has noted Egypt still surrendered a two-goal cushion regardless of the officiating.
- Switzerland's win over Colombia ends a 72-year wait for a World Cup quarterfinal — their only prior appearance at this stage came as hosts in 1954. Goalkeeper Gregor Kobel credited the team's mentality for holding firm through a hostile road atmosphere and a squad missing several regulars.
- Didier Deschamps fielded more questions about his own future than his lineup on Wednesday — the 1998-winning captain and 14-year France coach has said this tournament will be his last in charge, regardless of today's result.
- French authorities are deploying several thousand police officers ahead of today's match, a precaution stemming from the unrest that followed France's 2022 semifinal win over Morocco.
