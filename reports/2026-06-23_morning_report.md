## TODAY'S MATCHES

**Portugal vs. Uzbekistan — 1:00 PM ET | NRG Stadium, Houston | Group K**
Portugal drew 1–1 with DR Congo on matchday one, which is the kind of opener that makes a second match feel considerably more loaded than it should. Portugal's projected XI runs a 4–3–3 with Costa; Cancelo, Rúben Dias, Inácio, Nuno Mendes; João Neves, Vitinha, Bruno Fernandes; Bernardo Silva, Ronaldo, João Félix — but Rúben Dias missed the DR Congo match and his availability today shapes Portugal's defensive structure. Uzbekistan are ranked 50th and making their World Cup debut; they lost 1–3 to Colombia in their opener but Fayzullaev scored their first-ever World Cup goal, which confirms they are not completely inert. The betting angle is Portugal covering a spread here — -475 on the ML offers no value, but the price of winning by two or more is where the edge lives.

**England vs. Ghana — 4:00 PM ET | Gillette Stadium, Boston | Group L**
England are heavy favorites at -450 and coming off a 4–2 win over Croatia that was more convincing than the scoreline suggests, given Croatia scored two in the first half before England adjusted at the break. Saka (Achilles), Rashford (hamstring discomfort), and Declan Rice (hamstring tightness) all trained Monday and are expected to be available, though Tuchel has hinted Madueke keeps the right wing start. Ghana are without Kudus (quadriceps), Salisu, Djiku, Lamptey, and Andre Ayew, which depletes their defensive spine and attacking threat simultaneously. Thomas Partey missed Ghana's opener against Panama due to a visa issue entering Canada but is expected to be available for today's match in Boston. England should win comfortably; the question is the margin and whether prop angles on Kane or Bellingham carry value.

**Panama vs. Croatia — 7:00 PM ET | BMO Field, Toronto | Group L**
Croatia drew 4–2 with England on matchday one — a scoreline that flatters them slightly given how England controlled proceedings from halftime onward — and need a win here to stay on track for the knockouts. Luka Modrić is still the engine of this side at whatever age we're pretending he is, and Panama are a functional CONCACAF side with limited attacking ceiling. Panama's projected setup is a 3–4–2–1 with Mosquera in goal and a midfield built around Carrasquilla. Croatia at -185 reflects their technical superiority, and the Toronto venue at sea level removes any environmental edge. The spread at +135 is more interesting than the ML.

**Colombia vs. DR Congo — 10:00 PM ET | Estadio Akron, Guadalajara | Group K**
Colombia won their opener 3–1 against Uzbekistan, with Luis Díaz and James Rodríguez pulling strings, and enter as moderate favorites at -195. DR Congo are led by captain Cédric Bakambu, Chancel Mbemba, and Fiston Mayele, and they earned their first-ever World Cup point against Portugal — a result that tells you more about Portugal's second-half complacency than DR Congo's ceiling. Estadio Akron in Guadalajara sits at roughly 5,100 feet of altitude, which structurally suppresses goal volume and second-half tempo. With both sides carrying Matchday 1 points, this match has the profile of a cautious opener-sequel — neither team is desperate yet.

## AROUND THE TOURNAMENT

- Messi scored a double against Austria to reach 18 World Cup goals, overtaking Klose and clearing Marta's women's record in the same afternoon. He missed a penalty in the 8th minute — the kind of miss that would be career-defining for anyone else — before curling in the record-breaker on 38 minutes and adding a late second. He turned 39 yesterday. The sport is occasionally obliging with its timing.

- After scoring twice against Austria, Messi told reporters his first-goal tears "weren't related to soccer" — his father Jorge has been receiving medical treatment this week. He's scored five goals across the first two matches while managing a family health situation from a different continent. The GOAT debate has cleared up.

- France's Philadelphia match was halted for 2 hours and 10 minutes at halftime due to storms soaking the Delaware Valley, and France came out and added two more anyway. There is something instructive about that, though it mostly tells you Iraq are not very good.

- Norway's fans exited MetLife in the rain in jubilant defiance after Haaland scored twice for the second consecutive match. Haaland saluted Messi on Snapchat with "Messi is a madman" after the Argentina game. He was also spotted at the Stanley Cup Final earlier this week. It appears Erling Haaland is having the best summer of anyone in North America and is choosing to document it accordingly.

- Concession prices at this World Cup deserve their own filing: $75 caviar-topped tots were reportedly spotted in Miami. A day's pay in beer in Mexico City. Norway fans were paying whatever it cost and didn't seem to mind. The tournament is generating revenue at a scale that suggests FIFA are not particularly concerned about the optics.
