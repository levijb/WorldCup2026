## Today's Matches

**England vs. DR Congo — FINAL: England 2–1 | Mercedes-Benz Stadium, Atlanta | Round of 32**
DR Congo opened the scoring in the 7th minute through Brian Cipenga — the first time England had fallen behind in this tournament — and goalkeeper Lionel Mpasi delivered one of the saves performances of the round, keeping England out repeatedly before Kane's 75th-minute header and 86th-minute strike sealed the comeback. Kane's 13th World Cup goal, breaking a tie with Pelé. England face Mexico in the Round of 16 at the Azteca, a fixture that will be played in front of 80,000+ partisan Mexican supporters. *(No bets — match complete.)*

**Belgium vs. Senegal — 4:00 PM ET | Lumen Field, Seattle | Round of 32**
The De Bruyne vs. Lukaku decision is Belgium's defining selection call this tournament: De Ketelaere is projected as the false nine with Lukaku off the bench, but Belgium have looked toothless in matches where they lack a genuine penalty-box presence — the two draws with Egypt and Iran were exactly that. Senegal arrive having scraped through on a 5–0 Iraq result inflated by a 13th-minute red card, and goalkeeper Edouard Mendy (knee) is out, replaced by Mory Diaw. Lumen Field is sea-level and temperate — neutral conditions. Senegal's counter-attack through Mané, Sarr, and Ndiaye is the live threat against a Belgian high line, and Senegal have scored 10 of their last 12 goals in the second half, which suggests the longer this stays level, the better their chances get. Belgium are priced as slight favorites at +110 ML despite being the group winner — a line that reflects genuine uncertainty, not public sentiment.

**USA vs. Bosnia and Herzegovina — 8:00 PM ET | Levi's Stadium, Santa Clara | Round of 32**
Pulisic told reporters on Tuesday that he's "feeling good" after his calf injury and is expected to start — his first from kickoff since the Paraguay opener where he was hurt. The USA's right-sided machine (Freeman, Dest, McKennie) has been the engine of this team, and they get Bosnia, who have the tournament's worst defensive record among sides that advanced: six matches and zero clean sheets in World Cup history. Edin Dzeko at 40 is still Bolivia-sized aerially and teenager Kerim Alajbegovic has been Bosnia's best player, but their defensive system wobbles under sustained pressure. Bosnia's Tarik Muharemovic returns from suspension to stabilize their backline — the most meaningful lineup difference from their group-stage form. Levi's Stadium is a controlled dome environment. The public is heavy on USA (-265 ML), which compresses value; the spread or a USA-focused total are the smarter angles.

## Predictions

#### England vs. DR Congo *(Final: England 2–1)*

*Match complete — see Results above.*

#### Belgium vs. Senegal

Belgium's best football comes when De Bruyne and Doku have a genuine central target to aim at, and the debate about Lukaku's deployment has followed this team all tournament. With De Ketelaere in the false nine role, Belgium's movement is fluid but their box presence is reduced — the two draws in the group stage came in exactly that configuration. Senegal's defensive record is mixed: they've given up chances against everyone but compensate with individual moments and the pace of Mané on the counter. Diaw replacing Mendy is the most significant lineup variable, and it creates a real question about Senegal's ability to hold leads if Belgium generate sustained quality inside the box.

Belgium's attacking talent should eventually wear down a Senegal side that has not kept a clean sheet in this tournament, but Senegal have shown they can score at any moment and a level scoreline deep in the second half gives them their best chance — either a late goal to steal it or a shootout, where Courtois is an advantage. Belgium edge a close match if Lukaku comes off the bench and provides the decisive penalty-box moment. Predicted score: 2–1 (Belgium)

#### USA vs. Bosnia and Herzegovina

Bosnia's structure without the ball — a mid-block designed to frustrate through the middle and force play wide — has limited the goals they've conceded, but it also invites the USA's strength: overlapping runs from Freeman and Dest on the right side, and Pulisic (if fit for 90) creating in the channels. Edin Dzeko provides aerial presence from set pieces in both boxes, and Bosnia have proven they can score from nothing, but they've also never kept a World Cup clean sheet, which speaks to a defensive record built on minimizing damage rather than shutting teams out. Muharemovic returning from suspension adds the central defensive discipline Bosnia lacked in their weakest moments.

At home in front of a massive partisan crowd, with Adams and McKennie shielding the back four and Pulisic's return adding a dimension Bosnia's compact mid-block will struggle to neutralize in transition, the USA should control this and score through width and combination play. Bosnia get a goal — they always do — but the USA push through. Predicted score: 2–1 (USA)

## Around the Tournament

- Harry Kane scored his 12th and 13th World Cup goals today, breaking a tie with Pelé and moving into sole possession of 5th place on the all-time men's list. England now face Mexico at the Azteca — the same stadium where Kane's 2018 World Cup really began.
- Norway's goal celebrations have been registering on the University of Bergen's seismometer. When Haaland scored against Iraq on June 17, a distinct seismic spike was recorded. Researchers confirmed the vibrations matched the exact minute of the goal — fans celebrating in unison generate measurable ground movement. It happened again during the Ivory Coast match.
- Ronald Koeman resigned as Netherlands manager hours after the Morocco shootout defeat — sources confirm the decision was mutual and immediate. The Dutch Football Association has opened a search process.
- Mexico's second-half goals against Ecuador kept their no-concession run intact through all six games at this tournament and qualifying — a defensive record without precedent for El Tri, who will now host England at the Azteca in the Round of 16.
- Bosnia and Herzegovina's tournament represents their first World Cup knockout appearance since the nation's debut in 2014. Dubioza Kolektiv's "USA" has reportedly been playing from Bosnian fan sections at every match, including in the group stages, as a deliberately ironic banner given tonight's fixture.
