## Results

**Yesterday — Monday, June 29**

- Brazil 2–1 Japan (Round of 32) — Kaishu Sano put Japan ahead before the half-hour, Casemiro leveled after the break, and Gabriel Martinelli won it in stoppage time. Neymar didn't play a single minute. Brazil were second-best for long stretches and needed a late moment of quality to avoid the upset — efficient over dominant has been their pattern all tournament, and that gap is worth tracking into the Ivory Coast/Norway tie.

Tah then missed the penalty that sent Germany home — a brutal individual moment for a team that had won six of its previous seven shootouts in major tournaments. Bart Verbruggen's extra-time save kept the Netherlands alive before the shootout turned against them. Morocco's knockout-stage pedigree from Qatar 2022 continues to outperform their group-stage form.

## Today's Matches

**Ivory Coast vs. Norway — 1:00 PM ET | AT&T Stadium, Arlington | Round of 32**

Erling Haaland is back in the starting XI after Solbakken pulled him for the dead-rubber loss to France — a deliberate freshness call that now gets tested against a team that has conceded just once in 11 matches. Ivory Coast's defense, built around the duo that frustrated Germany for long stretches, is the best Norway have faced with their first-choice attack on the pitch. Norway's right-back spot is a live question: Julian Ryerson sat out the France match with a thigh injury and has missed training since, which would push Fredrik Aursnes into the back line if he's not cleared. Norway carry the talent edge with Haaland, Sørloth, and Ødegaard, but Ivory Coast have generated this tournament's most stubborn defensive numbers from a side outside the traditional contenders. AT&T Stadium's retractable roof neutralizes the Texas heat as a variable. The market leans Norway, but the gap between the two sides is closer than the price suggests.

**France vs. Sweden — 5:00 PM ET | MetLife Stadium, East Rutherford | Round of 32**

France enter as the single largest favorite of the round — a -400 line that reflects a perfect 9-point group stage, a +8 goal differential, and Mbappé and Dembélé both sitting on four goals apiece. Sweden's actual threat is real: Gyökeres and Isak form a Premier League-caliber strike pair, and Graham Potter's side scored in every group match. The problem is the other end — Sweden have kept zero clean sheets in their last nine matches across all competitions and conceded five to a Netherlands side that isn't appreciably better than France. Isak Hien is ruled out for the tournament with a hamstring injury, thinning an already shaky Swedish back line, and Victor Lindelöf moves from midfield to cover. William Saliba is probable for France despite a minor fitness note. This is the round's clearest mismatch on paper, with the only live question being the margin and whether Sweden's attack gets enough service to make it a goals game rather than a rout.

**Mexico vs. Ecuador — 9:00 PM ET | Estadio Azteca, Mexico City | Round of 32**

Mexico are unbeaten and unscored-on through three group games, a defensive record built on a back four that hasn't conceded since the tournament opened. Ecuador arrive on the opposite kind of momentum — a comeback win over Germany that secured their place as a best-third-place qualifier and validated a defense anchored by Pacho, Hincapié, and Caicedo, three of the tournament's better young defenders. Raúl Jiménez returns to lead the Mexico line after being rested in the Czechia dead rubber, and Gilberto Mora's inclusion remains the swing factor in whether Mexico's attack finds the spaces a well-organized Ecuador side is built to deny. Estadio Azteca sits at roughly 7,300 feet, a genuine aerobic factor that historically suppresses second-half tempo and favors the side that controls territory early — which, combined with two defenses that have been hard to break down all tournament, points toward a low-event match. With both sides clean on the injury front, this projects as the tightest and lowest-scoring match of the day.

## Predictions

#### Ivory Coast vs. Norway

Norway's attacking ceiling with Haaland, Sørloth, and Ødegaard all available is higher than anything Ivory Coast have shown they can be exposed to this tournament, but Ivory Coast's defensive shape has been built specifically to deny exactly the kind of direct, high-tempo service that fed Haaland's four group-stage goals. The right-back question for Norway is more than a footnote — if Ryerson is unavailable, Aursnes shifting from midfield weakens Norway's ball progression in a match where they may need to break down a low block for long stretches. Ivory Coast's route to a result runs through patience and set pieces rather than open exchanges, which favors a tighter match than Norway's group-stage scoring rate would suggest.

This has the shape of a match decided by one moment rather than sustained pressure — Haaland needs only a sliver of space to convert, and Ivory Coast know it, which should produce a cagier first hour than either side's attacking talent implies. Predicted score: 2–1 (Norway)

#### France vs. Sweden

France's group-stage numbers — 4.91 xG, 48 shots, 91 box touches — describe a team that has been closing matches out with room to spare, and Sweden's defensive record (zero clean sheets in nine matches, 30 goals conceded in 14) is precisely the profile that gets exploited by a side with this much attacking depth. Sweden's path to relevance runs entirely through Gyökeres and Isak on the counter, but France's midfield control through Tchouaméni and Rabiot should limit the transition windows that kind of strike pair needs to function. The absence of Isak Hien removes Sweden's best defensive organizer at the worst possible time against this opponent.

France should establish control early and the only live question is whether Sweden's attacking talent is enough to make this a goals-both-ends match rather than a clean-sheet rout — recent French form says it won't be a clean sheet, but it will be a comfortable French win regardless. Predicted score: 4–1 (France)

#### Mexico vs. Ecuador

Both sides have built their tournaments on defensive solidity rather than attacking flair, which sets up a match where territorial control and set-piece quality matter more than individual brilliance. Mexico's full 90-minute home support and Jiménez's return to the lineup give them a slight edge in generating the kind of crosses and second-ball situations that have produced their goals so far, but Ecuador's central defensive pairing of Pacho and Hincapié has been excellent enough this tournament that individual Mexican movement alone likely isn't enough to break through. The altitude at Azteca compounds as the match wears on, and a team built around discipline and structure — which describes both sides here — tends to benefit from that more than one needing to chase a game.

This has the profile of a match that stays level deep into the second half before one set piece or moment of quality decides it, with Mexico's territorial edge and home environment giving them the marginal advantage to find it first. Predicted score: 1–0 (Mexico)

## Around the Tournament

- Paraguay's win over Germany is the biggest upset of the tournament by FIFA ranking gap — 34th taking down 12th. José Canale, who only started because Omar Alderete was injured against Australia, scored the winning penalty. Manuel Neuer saved a Germany penalty in the shootout and Germany still lost, with Jonathan Tah's skied sudden-death attempt the difference.
- DR Congo's "Lumumba" superfan — Michel Nkuka Mboladinga, who stands as a living statue throughout matches in tribute to the country's first prime minister — has become one of the tournament's recurring images and will be back for Wednesday's Round of 32 match against England.
- Seattle's hosting numbers from the closed group stage: four Fan Celebrations drew over 750,000 visitors, and The Athletic ranked Lumen Field the No. 1 stadium experience among all World Cup venues. The city now turns to its first knockout match on July 1.
- A wedding party in Philadelphia got swallowed by a Croatian fan march outside City Hall during the group stage — the crowd shifted from football chants to wedding songs mid-celebration, and the newlyweds reportedly loved it.
- Didier Deschamps, reportedly in his final tournament after 14 years as France manager, returned to the squad following his mother's funeral and hasn't missed a beat — France haven't conceded more than once in any competitive match this tournament since his return.
