## Today's Matches

**Croatia vs. Ghana — 5:00 PM ET | Lincoln Financial Field, Philadelphia | Group L**

This is a winner-takes-second match with Ghana holding the head-to-head edge on goal difference. Croatia have been tactically competent but have not generated the volume needed to take games over — they beat Panama 1–0 with minimal margin. Ghana's WC2026 form shows one win (Ghana 1–0 Panama) and one draw (England 0–0 Ghana), a side that defends intelligently and counter-attacks through Antoine Semenyo and Ernest Nuamah in the absence of injured Mohammed Kudus. The key matchup is whether Luka Modrić can control the midfield tempo in a match Croatia need to win, against a Ghana press that has been physical and disruptive. Philadelphia is a dome-free open stadium; weather is not a factor. Croatia need to attack, Ghana can sit and break — this suits Ghana more than the lineup suggests.

**Panama vs. England — 5:00 PM ET | MetLife Stadium, East Rutherford, NJ | Group L**

Reece James is out with a hamstring injury sustained in the Ghana draw and is confirmed a doubt for this match and potentially the Round of 32 — Djed Spence or Jarell Quansah likely covers at right back. Declan Rice has a calf issue and missed training; he may be rested with one booking away from a suspension that would cost him the Round of 32 entirely. Bukayo Saka is expected to start for the first time this tournament after being managed carefully through Matchdays 1 and 2. England have the quality to dismantle a Panama block — Harry Kane missed a crucial chance vs. Ghana and is overdue — but the 0–0 against Ghana exposed real creative limitations against deep defensive structures. Panama have never kept a World Cup clean sheet in nine attempts, and their xGA of 2.90 across this tournament is higher than their goals conceded.

**Colombia vs. Portugal — 7:30 PM ET | Hard Rock Stadium, Miami | Group K**

The most watchable match of the day. Colombia need only a draw to top Group K; Portugal must win to take the group. Both advance regardless, so the knockout bracket path is the real prize here — Group K winners get a third-place team from Groups D/E/I/J/L in the Round of 32, the runner-up gets the Group L second-place team (likely Croatia or Ghana). Colombia have won both games by playing high-tempo, aggressive football with Daniel Muñoz contributing two goals from right back; James Rodríguez and Luis Díaz in front of him is a real combination. Portugal come in with momentum after the 5–0 over Uzbekistan and Ronaldo's two-goal game, but Tomás Araújo remains a doubt at center back, leaving Renato Veiga alongside Rúben Dias. Bruno Fernandes has acknowledged the crowd will be majority Colombian in Miami — this match may functionally be a home game for Colombia. Hard Rock Stadium is sea-level, no altitude or heat dome concerns.

**DR Congo vs. Uzbekistan — 7:30 PM ET | Mercedes-Benz Stadium, Atlanta | Group K**

The stakes are real for DR Congo (1pt, -1 GD): a win puts them at 4pts and into third-place contention, potentially leapfrogging teams currently in the bubble. Uzbekistan are eliminated from all automatic spots but have shown they can score — they put one past Colombia and Portugal before conceding five. DR Congo's tournament has been defined by a defensive shape that frustrated Portugal early before conceding; their attacking output is limited. This match may come down to DR Congo's discipline and ability to create on limited possession. Atlanta's dome venue means controlled conditions. The main variable is whether DR Congo can find the goals — their attack has been their weak link all group stage.

**Algeria vs. Austria — 10:00 PM ET | Arrowhead Stadium, Kansas City | Group J**

The tensest match of the day structurally. Both teams on 3pts; a draw sends Austria through as Group J second-place on goal difference and leaves Algeria scrambling for a third-place slot where their -2 GD makes their path precarious. Algeria striker Mohammed Amoura (hamstring) remains out — Riyad Mahrez becomes their entire creative output, and he is 35 years old on his third World Cup. Austria's Paul Wanner is managing a calf knock. Ralf Rangnick is reportedly rotating his forward line and may deploy Arnautovic from the start after his impact as a substitute. The draw at +120 is the sharpest line on the board today — both teams know the math. Algeria need a win and cannot afford passive play; Austria need nothing more than a draw. That asymmetry usually produces a cagey match with one side sitting.

**Jordan vs. Argentina — 10:00 PM ET | AT&T Stadium, Arlington (Dallas) | Group J**

Full dead rubber for Argentina. They face Cape Verde in the Round of 32 regardless of tonight's result. Scaloni will rotate — Cristian Romero is listed as a doubt after picking up an injury vs. Austria, and Messi's minute management is the central question. Jordan lost 2–1 to Algeria in their last match but showed competitive stretches; Jordan's first-ever WC goal came in this tournament. This match has no betting significance for the primary result market — the live rotation risk makes it unpropable across the board. The only value is Jordan covering a large spread if Argentina field a B lineup.

## Predictions

**Croatia vs. Ghana**

Ghana have been the more consistent defensive unit across this group — they neutralized England and held their shape when Panama attacked — while Croatia have needed maximum effort to win 1–0 against a limited opponent. Modrić remains the fulcrum, but at 39, his ability to dictate for 90 minutes in a must-win is not automatic, especially against a Ghana midfield that will press early and take away his time on the ball. Croatia's wide play through Ivan Perišić will be their primary route, but without Kudus, Ghana's defensive shape is actually cleaner — fewer counter-threats to chase means more compact tracking. Semenyo's pace on the counter is Ghana's main weapon, and he only needs one.

Croatia need to attack to advance, which means space. Ghana will invite pressure, stay compact, and play one moment through Semenyo or Nuamah. Croatia find a goal but cannot hold a lead against a team with this defensive organization. Predicted score: 1–1 (Draw)

**Panama vs. England**

England's creative problem is documented now: in the 0–0 vs. Ghana they had possession, shots, and nothing through a low block. Saka returning to the starting lineup changes the dynamic — he creates from wide with lateral movement that Madueke and Gordon did not provide, and he earns fouls. Kane needs goals; Panama have conceded in 100% of their World Cup history. The key question is whether Rice starts or is rested; if he sits, Bellingham runs the midfield freely and that accelerates England's control. Panama will sit in a 5–3–2 low block and be physical — the first goal is worth two in this match structure.

England get the first goal inside 60 minutes, Panama's compactness loosens, and England add a second late. A convincing performance that settles Tuchel's lineup questions heading into the Round of 32. Predicted score: 3–0 (England)

**Colombia vs. Portugal**

Colombia's 6-point group stage has been built on speed of transition, James Rodríguez pulling strings from the ten position, and Muñoz as a high-value attacking full-back. Portugal have not been tested by a side this athletic — Uzbekistan and DR Congo are different propositions. The Ronaldo factor is real; he is in form and will be well-served if Fernandes finds him in the box, but Davinson Sánchez and Lucumí have been excellent this tournament and are not going to be caught by individual Ronaldo runs. If Bernardo Silva starts over one of the wide players, Portugal have the craft to open Colombia centrally.

Colombia's need for only a draw will make them pragmatic in possession and dangerous on the break. Portugal must chase the game if they go behind, which creates space for Colombian transitions. The most likely result in a match where one side needs only to avoid defeat. Predicted score: 1–1 (Draw)

**DR Congo vs. Uzbekistan**

DR Congo have the better squad across the pitch, the higher stakes, and an opponent that is both eliminated and defensively open. Uzbekistan proved they can score — both Portugal and Colombia leaked to them — but DR Congo's defensive shape was strong enough to frustrate Portugal for an hour. The question is whether DR Congo can generate the final third quality to convert, something they have been light on. Under Sébastien Desabre they have played a patient, compact shape that is hard to score against but also slow to manufacture goals.

DR Congo grind this out with a goal from a set piece or punishing a mistake. Uzbekistan press forward looking for a consolation win and leave gaps. Predicted score: 2–0 (DR Congo)

**Algeria vs. Austria**

The math makes this almost structurally a draw: Austria actively benefit from not losing, Algeria must win or their third-place position is fragile. With Amoura out, Algeria's attacking weight shifts entirely to Mahrez — and Rangnick's defensive shape (which frustrated Argentina before Messi's individual brilliance) is built to funnel play away from wide runners like him. Austria will sit at 4–4–2 in their own half and absorb, then hit on the counter through Arnautovic. Algeria will have the ball but struggle to find the vertical passes that unlock a disciplined Austrian block. Expect cards — this has a physical, contested midfield with both teams' knockout lives on the line.

Algeria press, Austria absorb. The stalemate logic is strong. If the draw is the base case, it takes one set piece or Mahrez moment to change it. Predicted score: 1–1 (Draw)

**Jordan vs. Argentina**

Argentina rotate meaningfully. Scaloni will protect Messi's minutes — likely a 60-minute appearance at most — and rests Romero. Jordan's defensive shape has held up for stretches in this tournament; they scored against Algeria and Austria. This is not a 5–0. Argentina's second-string is still better than Jordan, but the motivation gap is significant. Jordan play for pride and their fans. Argentina's late goals will dry up without Messi for 90 minutes.

Argentina score early through whoever Scaloni starts, manage the game comfortably, allow Jordan a consolation. Messi gets 20–30 minutes and everyone walks away fine. Predicted score: 2–1 (Argentina)

## Around the Tournament

- Dembélé's hat trick with all three goals scored before the 33rd minute was the second-earliest from kick-off in World Cup history — only Austria's Erich Probst reached the milestone faster, doing so in 24 minutes against Czechoslovakia at the 1954 tournament. It was also the first first-half hat trick at a men's World Cup since Russia's Oleg Salenko in 1994 against Cameroon. Three hat tricks at a single World Cup is already a notable total: Messi, Canada's Jonathan David, and Dembélé have each done it.

- French fans in attendance displayed a banner reading "AVEC TOI DIDIER" — "With you, Didier" — acknowledging coach Deschamps, who missed the Norway match to be with family following his mother's death. His assistant Guy Stéphan ran the team. France won 4–1 and are back together ahead of the knockouts.

- The Golden Boot race is legitimately interesting: Messi leads with five goals through two matches; Vinicius Júnior, Haaland, Mbappé, and Dembélé each have four. Messi plays tonight in a dead rubber with rotation expected — the Golden Boot lead is fragile if he sits. Ronaldo is at two and needs a big game vs. Colombia to re-enter the conversation.

- In June 2026 the Canadian government refused to grant Ghana's Thomas Partey an entry visa because of criminal charges for rape and sexual assault filed against the player in the United Kingdom. The context is relevant today: Ghana face Croatia without both Kudus (quadriceps) and Partey (visa denial), removing their two most experienced central midfielders. That the Black Stars still have 4pts is a testament to Queiroz's organization — and a warning not to underestimate what they can protect.

- On June 22, Messi scored his 17th World Cup goal against Austria, passing Miroslav Klose, then added his 18th in the same match to surpass Marta and become the all-time leading World Cup scorer — men's or women's combined. He plays tonight in Arlington. He needs 20 minutes and one touch.
