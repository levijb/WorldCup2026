## Today's Matches

**Croatia vs. Ghana — 5:00 PM ET | Lincoln Financial Field, Philadelphia | Group L**

This is a winner-takes-second match with Ghana holding the head-to-head edge on goal difference. Croatia have been tactically competent but have not generated the volume needed to take games over — they beat Panama 1–0 with minimal margin. Ghana's WC2026 form shows one win (Ghana 1–0 Panama) and one draw (England 0–0 Ghana), a side that defends intelligently and counter-attacks through Antoine Semenyo and Ernest Nuamah in the absence of injured Mohammed Kudus. The key matchup is whether Luka Modrić can control the midfield tempo in a match Croatia need to win, against a Ghana press that has been physical and disruptive. Philadelphia is an open stadium; weather is not a factor. Croatia need to attack, Ghana can sit and break — this suits Ghana more than the lineup suggests. Note: Croatia finishing third here would also send them into the third-place bubble where they currently sit 5th (3pts, -1 GD) — a loss eliminates them entirely if Ghana go through.

England are -700 on the ML (bet365); the spread at -1.5 is the more useful angle. Kane is -185 anytime scorer at FanDuel.

The draw is +280 at bet365. Hard Rock Stadium is sea-level, no altitude or heat dome concerns.

Atlanta's dome venue means controlled conditions.

Note: a draw here also benefits Algeria as a third-place team given their current -2 GD; losing would almost certainly end their tournament.

## Predictions

**Croatia vs. Ghana**

Ghana have been the more consistent defensive unit across this group — they neutralized England and held their shape when Panama attacked — while Croatia have needed maximum effort to win 1–0 against a limited opponent. Modrić remains the fulcrum, but at 39, his ability to dictate for 90 minutes in a must-win is not automatic, especially against a Ghana midfield that will press early. Without Kudus and Partey, Ghana are missing their two most experienced central figures — yet Queiroz's organization has absorbed those absences for two matches already. Semenyo's pace on the counter is Ghana's main weapon, and he only needs one.

Croatia need to attack to advance, which means space. Ghana will invite pressure, stay compact, and play one moment through Semenyo or Nuamah. Croatia find a goal but cannot hold a lead against a team with this defensive organization. Predicted score: 1–1 (Draw)

**Panama vs. England**

England's creative problem is documented now: in the 0–0 vs. Ghana they had possession, shots, and nothing through a low block. Saka returning to the starting lineup changes the dynamic — he creates from wide with lateral movement that Madueke and Gordon did not provide, and he earns fouls. Kane needs goals; Panama have conceded in 100% of their World Cup history. The key question is whether Rice starts or is rested; if he sits, Bellingham runs the midfield freely and that accelerates England's control. Panama will sit in a 5–3–2 low block and be physical — the first goal is worth two in this match structure.

England get the first goal inside 60 minutes, Panama's compactness loosens, and England add a second late. A convincing performance that settles Tuchel's lineup questions heading into the Round of 32. Predicted score: 3–0 (England)

**Colombia vs. Portugal**

Colombia's 6-point group stage has been built on speed of transition, James Rodríguez pulling strings from the ten position, and Muñoz as a high-value attacking full-back. Portugal have not been tested by a side this athletic — Uzbekistan and DR Congo are different propositions. The Ronaldo factor is real; he is in form and will be well-served if Fernandes finds him in the box, but Davinson Sánchez and Lucumí have been excellent this tournament. Colombia's need for only a draw will make them pragmatic in possession and dangerous on the break. Portugal must chase the game if they go behind, which creates space for Colombian transitions. The most likely result in a match where one side needs only to avoid defeat. Predicted score: 1–1 (Draw)

**DR Congo vs. Uzbekistan**

DR Congo have the better squad across the pitch, the higher stakes, and an opponent that is both eliminated and defensively open. Uzbekistan proved they can score — both Portugal and Colombia leaked to them — but DR Congo's defensive shape was strong enough to frustrate Portugal for an hour. The question is whether DR Congo can generate the final third quality to convert, something they have been light on. DR Congo grind this out with a goal from a set piece or punishing a mistake. Uzbekistan press forward looking for a consolation win and leave gaps. Predicted score: 2–0 (DR Congo)

**Algeria vs. Austria**

The math makes this almost structurally a draw: Austria actively benefit from not losing, Algeria must win or their third-place position is fragile. With Amoura out, Algeria's attacking weight shifts entirely to Mahrez — and Rangnick's defensive shape is built to funnel play away from wide runners like him. Austria will sit deep and absorb, then hit on the counter through Arnautovic. Algeria will have the ball but struggle to find the vertical passes that unlock a disciplined Austrian block. Expect cards — this has a physical, contested midfield with both teams' knockout lives on the line. The draw logic is strong. If it breaks, it takes one Mahrez moment or Austrian set piece. Predicted score: 1–1 (Draw)

**Jordan vs. Argentina**

Argentina rotate meaningfully. Scaloni will protect Messi's minutes — likely a 60-minute appearance at most — and rests Romero. Jordan's defensive shape has held up for stretches in this tournament; they scored against Algeria and Austria. Argentina score early through whoever Scaloni starts, manage the game comfortably, allow Jordan a consolation. Messi gets 20–30 minutes and everyone walks away fine. Predicted score: 2–1 (Argentina)

## Around the Tournament

- Dembélé's hat trick with all three goals scored before the 33rd minute was the second-earliest from kick-off in World Cup history — only Austria's Erich Probst reached the milestone faster, doing so in 24 minutes against Czechoslovakia at the 1954 tournament. It was also the first first-half hat trick at a men's World Cup since Russia's Oleg Salenko in 1994 against Cameroon. Three hat tricks at a single World Cup is already a notable total for this edition: Messi, Canada's Jonathan David, and Dembélé have each done it.

- French fans in attendance displayed a banner reading "AVEC TOI DIDIER" — "With you, Didier" — acknowledging coach Deschamps, who missed the Norway match to be with family following his mother's death. His assistant Guy Stéphan ran the team. France won 4–1 and are back together ahead of the knockouts.

- The Golden Boot race is legitimately interesting: Messi leads with five goals through two matches; Vinicius Júnior, Haaland, Mbappé, and Dembélé each have four. Messi plays tonight in a dead rubber with rotation expected — the Golden Boot lead is fragile if he sits. Ronaldo is at two goals and needs a big game vs. Colombia to re-enter the conversation.

- Ghana face Croatia today without both Kudus (quadriceps) and Partey (Canadian entry visa denied due to pending UK criminal charges), removing their two most experienced central midfielders. That the Black Stars still have 4pts is a testament to Queiroz's organization — and a warning not to underestimate what they can protect.

- On June 22, Messi scored his 17th World Cup goal against Austria, passing Miroslav Klose, then added his 18th in the same match to surpass Marta and become the all-time leading World Cup scorer — men's or women's combined. He plays tonight in Arlington. He needs 20 minutes and one touch.
