## Today's Matches

<a name="norway-vs-england"></a>
**Norway vs. England — 5:00 PM ET | Hard Rock Stadium, Miami Gardens, Florida**
Haaland has scored 7 goals from 5.9 xG and put 12 of 18 shots on target, and Norway has conceded in every match this tournament — the whole tie turns on whether England's makeshift back line can keep him quiet for 90 minutes. Norway reached a first-ever World Cup quarterfinal by beating Cote d'Ivoire and Brazil, both 2-1, largely off individual moments from Haaland; England ground past DR Congo and survived 36-plus minutes with 10 men to beat Mexico, leaning on greater squad depth rather than control. That depth is being tested directly today — Jarell Quansah is suspended, Jordan Henderson is out for the tournament, Reece James still isn't training, and Marc Guehi is a fresh doubt with a hamstring strain, leaving Thomas Tuchel patching together a back line that's changed constantly through the knockouts. Miami's afternoon heat is a factor for both squads' depth in the second half. Both attacks have real, proven output — this reads as the highest-event match of the round, not a coin-flip on the scoreline so much as on how many goals it takes to settle.

<a name="argentina-vs-switzerland"></a>
**Argentina vs. Switzerland — 9:00 PM ET | Arrowhead Stadium, Kansas City, Missouri**
Messi has 8 goals — the most of any player this tournament — and Argentina have needed him to bail them out of two separate knockout-round scares, extra time against Cape Verde and a 2-0 deficit against Egypt. Switzerland reached this stage the hard way too, grinding out a scoreless 120 minutes against Colombia before winning on penalties, and they haven't conceded a goal in regulation across the entire knockout stage — though that came with just two shots on target allowed in 120 minutes against Colombia, a low bar given how little Colombia's specific attack ultimately produced. Switzerland's Johan Manzambi, their breakout scorer, remains out with a knee injury, thinning an attack that was already the least productive left in the bracket without him. Argentina is fully fit and the class gap is real, but Switzerland's Gregor Kobel and a compact, well-drilled shape mean this is more likely to be a patient, low-event match than a rout — the kind of game where Messi has specifically thrived all tournament.

## Predictions

<a name="predictions-norway-vs-england"></a>
#### Norway vs. England
Haaland has scored two goals himself in seven of his eight career World Cup matches and is the single biggest reason England can't afford a loose evening at the back, but this isn't a one-man Norway team — Martin Odegaard has three assists and is the creative hub behind almost everything they generate, with Alexander Sorloth and Antonio Nusa giving the attack more than one route to goal. England's issue is that its back four has been reshuffled all tournament and changes again today without Quansah, against a striker who's put 12 of 18 shots on target this summer. Kane, though, has 6 goals from 4.19 xG with 3 of them headers, and Jude Bellingham has been England's other reliable source of goals when matches get tight.

Norway has conceded in every match this tournament, which suggests England's attack should get its own chances even if the back line struggles, and Haaland dragging Norway level or ahead at some point in this match feels close to inevitable. England's greater overall depth — even patched together — and knockout-stage composure should be enough to get over the line, but Norway's ceiling is high enough that extra time is a real possibility if Haaland strikes late. Predicted score: 3–2 (England).

<a name="predictions-argentina-vs-switzerland"></a>
#### Argentina vs. Switzerland
Messi has scored in nine straight World Cup matches and drew the assist and the equalizer out of nothing against Egypt in the same four-minute span, and Switzerland's whole defensive plan runs through Granit Xhaka denying him time to turn and face goal in central areas. Argentina have needed exactly that kind of individual magic twice already this tournament — extra time against Cape Verde, a two-goal comeback against Egypt — while Switzerland's path has been about defensive resilience rather than control, allowing just two shots on target in 120 minutes against Colombia while generating very little going forward themselves, especially without Manzambi. Julian Alvarez and Lautaro Martinez give Argentina more attacking options than Messi alone, but neither has been prolific this tournament, which keeps the scoring burden concentrated on the captain.

Switzerland's best path is staying compact and physical for as long as possible and hoping Kobel — who kept them alive through the entire Colombia match — can do it again, but Argentina's individual quality and superior overall depth should tell in the second half even if the first is cagey. A repeat of the extra-time drama that's defined Argentina's tournament is a live possibility given how well-organized Switzerland has been defensively all knockout stage. Predicted score: 2–0 (Argentina).

## Around the Tournament

- Jude Bellingham and Erling Haaland were teammates and close friends at Borussia Dortmund for two seasons, sharing 63 appearances and a 2021 German Cup — today's quarterfinal is the first time they've ever faced each other competitively.
- Spain's Mikel Merino has now scored the winning goal off the bench in back-to-back knockout matches, against Portugal and now Belgium, both after coming on in the second half.
- Belgium's Thibaut Courtois left the Spain match with a thigh injury in the second half, forced to hand over to reserve keeper Senne Lammens, whose error led directly to Spain's winner.
- A charity-calendar photo of a young Lionel Messi bathing a baby in 2007 has resurfaced across social media this week — that baby grew up to be Lamine Yamal, who played against Messi's Argentina teammates in the same World Cup nearly two decades later.
- Norway's run has been powered almost entirely by one player, but Stale Solbakken's side has already guaranteed the deepest tournament run in its history regardless of tonight's result.
