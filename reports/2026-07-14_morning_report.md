- Day 34 — Semifinals, Match 1 of 2. France vs. Spain kicks off today; England vs. Argentina follows tomorrow.
- 4 teams remain — France, Spain, England, Argentina — and all four have won a World Cup before. First all-champion final four since 1990.

## Today's Matches

**France vs. Spain — 3:00 PM ET | AT&T Stadium, Arlington**
This is the tightest match of the tournament by the numbers — Spain actually leads France in expected goal differential per 90 (+1.56 to +1.43) and owns the competition's stingiest defense (0.64 xGA/90). France arrives unbeaten in six matches (16 goals, +14 GD), a run capped by a 2-0 quarterfinal win over Morocco, while Spain needed a late Mikel Merino goal to see off Belgium 2-1 in the quarters — the only match all tournament in which Spain has conceded. Kylian Mbappé is cleared after an ankle knock against Morocco, and Tchouaméni and Kone are both back from minor issues; Spain is close to full strength, with Lamine Yamal now fully integrated after early fitness question marks. AT&T Stadium's retractable roof keeps this climate-controlled regardless of the 95°F+ Texas heat outside, so conditions aren't a factor. Two units built on suffocating opponents rather than getting stretched themselves — this reads like a single-goal, low-event match rather than a repeat of last year's 5-4 Nations League track meet between these same two sides.

## Predictions

<a name="predictions-france-vs-spain"></a>
#### France vs. Spain

Spain's defense has been this tournament's best by the numbers — a 0.64 xGA/90 that cracked only once, when Belgium's direct route-one balls exposed a surprisingly slow center-back pairing in Pau Cubarsí and Aymeric Laporte. That's the blueprint for France to attack: Deschamps' side leads the tournament in fast breaks (2.17/match) and direct attacks (3.67/match), and Mbappé and Dembélé have the pace to run in behind the same way Belgium did. Spain, in turn, has to break down a France defense that's conceded twice all tournament, both in the group stage — Rodri and Pedri need to control tempo and funnel service to Yamal and Oyarzabal rather than trying to walk the ball in, the approach that got stonewalled by Portugal- and Paraguay-level low blocks. This is a matchup of two units that have spent the tournament suffocating opponents rather than getting stretched themselves, and neither figures to break that pattern easily.

Expect a cagey, low-event first half — this tournament's knockout matches have gone under 1.5 first-half goals in 23 of 28 games, and neither of these disciplined units figures to be the exception. The match likely turns on one moment: a Mbappé or Dembélé breakaway, a Merino-style super-sub intervention, or a set piece given the expanded VAR scope. Predicted score: 1–1 (Draw — goes to ET/PKs, France advances).

<a name="around-the-tournament"></a>
## Around the Tournament

- This is the first semifinal since 1990 in which all four remaining teams have previously won the World Cup — and the first time the final four also matches the pre-tournament top four in the FIFA rankings.
- The Golden Boot race is a dead heat: Mbappé and Messi are both on 8 goals, with Mbappé holding the tiebreak lead on assists (3-2) as the two stars enter their respective semifinals a day apart.
- Unai Simón set a World Cup-record 649-minute shutout streak for Spain before Belgium finally broke through in the quarterfinal — the same match that ended Spain's six-match clean-sheet run.
- France is bidding to become just the third nation to reach three consecutive World Cup finals, joining Brazil (1994-2002) and West Germany (1982-1990).
- Spain, the reigning European champion, is trying to become just the third team since 1974 to reach a World Cup semifinal as sitting Euros winner — a feat last managed by Spain itself in 2010.
