## Predictions

<a name="predictions-england-vs-argentina"></a>
#### England vs. Argentina

Argentina's fullback pairing of Nahuel Molina and Nicolás Tagliafico has been the clearest structural weakness among this tournament's final four — Cape Verde and Egypt both created real problems in the wide channels, and neither man profiles as an elite one-on-one defender. England's width is built to test exactly that: Bukayo Saka against Tagliafico, Anthony Gordon against Molina. On the other side, Declan Rice's central presence has to cut off the Messi–Enzo Fernández–Mac Allister rotation that dictates Argentina's tempo, and Messi has been more creator than finisher lately — directly involved in 10 of Argentina's 17 goals this tournament, but without a goal of his own in the quarterfinal. Both defenses have been shakier than their unbeaten records suggest: England has conceded in three straight matches behind a back four that's changed personnel every game, and Argentina needed extra time in two of its last three matches to escape.

Expect this to open up more than the market's total implies — both attacks have been among the final four's most productive, and neither defense has looked fully settled under pressure. Argentina's big-match pedigree and Emiliano Martínez's shootout record make this a genuine coin flip if it goes the distance, but England's width advantage should tell in open play before then. Predicted score: 2–1 (England win).

## Around the Tournament

- Lamine Yamal turned 19 the day before facing France — and delivered the play that won Spain's opening penalty less than 24 hours into his 20th year.
- The Golden Boot race lost half its co-leadership overnight: Mbappé's tournament ended goalless in the semifinal, still tied with Messi at 8 — but Messi gets one more match to break the deadlock.
- Today's match is the first-ever meeting between Lionel Messi and England in his international career, despite 205 caps and 125 goals for Argentina.
- England and Argentina meet for the first time in over two decades, reviving a rivalry that includes the 1986 "Hand of God" quarterfinal and a penalty-shootout heartbreak in 1998.
- Sunday's final is set on one side regardless of today's result: Spain, chasing a second title 16 years after its only one, against either an England side seeking its first final since 1966 or an Argentina side chasing back-to-back titles.
