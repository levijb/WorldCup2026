## Tournament Status

- **Day 25 — Round of 16, day 2 of 4.** Two matches today; four more follow through Monday and Tuesday to complete the round.
- **Yesterday's results both went to the pre-match favorite** — Morocco routed Canada 3-0, ending the first co-host's tournament, while France ground past Paraguay 1-0 in an increasingly ill-tempered match. No upsets yet in the Round of 16.

<a name="results"></a>
## Results

**Yesterday — Saturday, July 4**
- Canada 0–3 Morocco — Canada dominated the first half and outshot Morocco 8-4 but managed only 0.78 xG; Azzedine Ounahi's second-half brace and a stoppage-time Soufiane Rahimi goal — both assisted by Brahim Diaz, now the all-time leading African assist-provider in World Cup history with four this tournament — ended the co-hosts' historic run.
- Paraguay 0–1 France (Mbappe 70' pen) — A fiery, cagey match settled by a VAR-awarded penalty after Desire Doue was fouled in the box; neither side had a shot on target in the first half, only the third World Cup knockout match since 1966 without one, and Paraguay picked up zero cards despite a series of late, niggling fouls that drew heavy media criticism.

**Two Days Ago — Friday, July 3**
- Australia 1–1 Egypt, Egypt win 4–2 on penalties — Salah's shootout Panenka sent Egypt into the Round of 16 for the first time ever; their only previous appearances (1934, 1990, 2018) never produced a World Cup knockout-stage win.
- Argentina 3–2 Cape Verde (AET) — Cape Verde leveled twice before a 111th-minute own goal off a Messi corner ended their historic debut run.
- Colombia 1–0 Ghana — Jhon Arias's early goal was enough against a Ghana side held to zero shots across 90 minutes.

## Today's Matches

<a name="brazil-vs-norway"></a>
**Brazil vs. Norway — 4:00 PM ET | MetLife Stadium, East Rutherford, New Jersey**
Brazil have found the net in every match this tournament but the underlying picture is shakier than the results suggest — they were second-best for large stretches against Japan before Gabriel Martinelli's stoppage-time winner, and Ancelotti now has to reshuffle without two of his most reliable creative outlets. Brazil topped Group A (draw Morocco, wins over Haiti and Scotland) before beating Japan 2-1 in the Round of 32, while Norway took Group I as a genuine attacking force (wins over Iraq and Senegal, a dead-rubber loss to France with players rested) before Erling Haaland's 86th-minute winner saw off Ivory Coast. Lucas Paqueta appears to have suffered a tournament-ending injury sustained against Japan, and Raphinha is confirmed out entirely, leaving Danilo Santos to fill Brazil's creative gap; Norway's only concern is Julian Ryerson (thigh), with Marcus Pedersen deputizing at right-back. MetLife is a standard sea-level, temperate venue — nothing structural to factor in. Haaland has scored in every match he's started this tournament, and with Brazil already missing two attacking pieces, this projects as a genuine two-way contest rather than the formality the moneyline implies.

<a name="mexico-vs-england"></a>
**Mexico vs. England — 8:00 PM ET | Estadio Azteca, Mexico City**
Mexico haven't conceded a single goal in four matches, and now England has to try to change that 7,200 feet above sea level in front of 87,000 fans who haven't seen their team lose a competitive home match since 2013. Mexico swept their group and beat Ecuador 2-0 in the Round of 32 (two first-half goals inside 31 minutes), while England topped Group L unbeaten before needing a stunning Harry Kane double — an equalizer and an 86th-minute winner — to escape DR Congo 2-1 in the Round of 32 after trailing inside 7 minutes. Reece James and Jarell Quansah are both still questionable at right-back, leaving England to patch the position again, most likely with Djed Spence; Mexico reports no fresh injury concerns beyond precautionary substitutions against Ecuador. The altitude is the single biggest variable in the tie — Tuchel himself has said England "cannot physically adapt to it" — and forecasts call for mild temperatures with a chance of thunderstorms rather than heat, meaning thin air rather than heat is the physical tax here. England's individual quality (Kane, Bellingham) gives them the tools to win, but Mexico's clean sheet streak and home advantage make this the tightest match on the board today.

## Predictions

<a name="predictions-brazil-vs-norway"></a>
#### Brazil vs. Norway
Brazil's attack has found the net in every match this tournament, but the underlying picture is uneven — they were second-best for long stretches against Japan before a stoppage-time Martinelli winner, and Ancelotti now has to solve that without Raphinha (confirmed out) or Lucas Paqueta (tournament-ending injury), his two most reliable creative outlets beyond Vinicius Jr. Norway counter with the single most dangerous individual weapon left in the bracket: Haaland has scored in every match he's started, his 5 goals arriving from almost exactly 5 expected goals — no fluke, just consistently elite finishing — and Norway's aerial route through Haaland and Sorloth targets precisely the space Brazil's attacking fullbacks vacate. Vinicius Jr's own numbers are comparable in scale (10 shots on target, tournament-leading shot involvements per 90), so this genuinely projects as end-to-end rather than one-sided.

Norway have never lost to Brazil in four all-time meetings, including the famous 1998 comeback, and while history alone means little, the tactical matchup gives Norway more of a route to a result than the moneyline suggests. Brazil's greater overall depth and bench options should eventually tell, but a match this close on individual quality could easily go the distance. Predicted score: 2–1 (Brazil).

<a name="predictions-mexico-vs-england"></a>
#### Mexico vs. England
Mexico haven't conceded a goal in four matches, a record built as much on altitude and crowd as on structure, while England arrive with real statistical superiority in attack (2.13 expected goals per game against Mexico's 1.26) undercut by a genuinely chaotic right-back situation — Reece James and Jarell Quansah both still questionable, leaving makeshift options at exactly the spot Mexico's Quinones and Alvarado are built to attack. Kane has scored 5 goals from 3.27 expected goals this tournament, a modest but real overperformance, and remains England's most reliable route to goal regardless of who plays around him. The altitude is the genuine variable — not just a talking point but a specific, compounding physical tax that bites hardest exactly when England's questionable fitness at the back becomes most exposed, past the hour mark.

If Mexico can keep this level past 60 minutes, the thinning air starts working against England's ability to press and rotate, and the crowd noise alone has unsettled bigger sides than this one at the Azteca. England's individual quality gives them the tools to win regardless, but this is exactly the kind of match — tight margins, hostile conditions, a makeshift back line — that goes the distance. Predicted score: 1–1 (Draw — goes to ET/PKs, England advances).

## Around the Tournament

- Canada becomes the first tournament co-host eliminated, closing out a run that included the nation's first-ever World Cup win and first-ever knockout victory in the same tournament.
- The Paraguay-France finale turned ugly: zero cards shown to Paraguay despite what BBC pundits described as a series of cynical late fouls, and Mbappe refused to shake goalkeeper Orlando Gill's hand after a full-time confrontation between both squads.
- Mbappe's penalty was his 11th World Cup knockout-stage goal, a record no other player has come within three of, and made him the first player in tournament history to score three or more knockout-stage goals in three different World Cups.
- Morocco's win makes them the first African nation to reach back-to-back World Cup quarterfinals, setting up a rematch of the 2022 semifinal against France on July 9 — the game France won 2-0 en route to that year's final.
- The Golden Boot race is tied at the top for the first time this tournament: Messi and Mbappe both sit on 7 goals, with Mbappe holding the tiebreaker lead on assists.
