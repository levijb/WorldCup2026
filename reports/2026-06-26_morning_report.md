## Today's Matches

**Norway vs. France — 3:00 PM ET | Gillette Stadium, Foxborough | Group I**
Both teams are already through, but the matchup decides seeding and knockout path — a meaningful prize in an expanded bracket where first place sets up a more favorable R32 draw. France's most significant problem heading into this match is William Saliba's doubtful status — if he doesn't play, Upamecano is exposed in one-on-one situations against the one team in this group built to exploit exactly that. Norway have scored seven goals in two matches on the back of Haaland (4 goals) and a pressing system that forces errors high up the pitch; France's line-setting without Saliba will be tested immediately. Tchouaméni anchors the French midfield and Mbappé leads the attack, but a France side without Saliba looks beatable against a team with Haaland's directness. Foxborough is a sea-level, standard-conditions venue — no environmental factor. The group-winner distinction is worth chasing for France, but Norway will not park; this is a genuine contest with genuine edge on the total.

**Senegal vs. Iraq — 3:00 PM ET | BMO Field, Toronto | Group I**
Both teams are eliminated and this is a dead rubber in the fullest sense. Senegal are playing without their starting goalkeeper Édouard Mendy (officially out) and Iraq have two forwards questionable (Al Mafraje, Al Shammari). France already cruised past Senegal 3–1 in Matchday 1 and Norway beat Iraq 4–1; neither team has found a result. Dead rubbers structurally favor draws and unders — expect rotation, low intensity, and a tepid first half. The only bet consideration here is the total.

**Cape Verde vs. Saudi Arabia — 8:00 PM ET | NRG Stadium, Houston | Group H**
Cape Verde need a win and for Uruguay to drop points to reach the knockouts. Saudi Arabia are playing for nothing — mathematically alive on paper (1pt) but eliminated for practical purposes given GD. The story of this match is whether Cape Verde, who drew Spain 0–0 and held a 2–2 with Uruguay, have enough juice to beat an opponent with nothing left to play for. Houston is a heat venue — afternoon index regularly above 95°F, but this kicks off at 8 PM ET (7 PM local), which provides some relief. Saudi Arabia have a confirmed starter: Sami Al-Dawsari in the left midfield role is their creative fulcrum. Cape Verde midfielder Borges Cabral is questionable; Tomas Gomes Arcanjo is also doubtful. The interesting line here is the spread, not the result, given that Cape Verde are playing with urgency and Saudi Arabia are not.

**Uruguay vs. Spain — 8:00 PM ET | Estadio Akron, Guadalajara | Group H**
The most tactically interesting match of the day. Uruguay must win to lock second place; a draw could still be enough if Cape Verde lose, but they cannot rely on that. Spain are already through as group winners and have incentive to rest legs — but Lamine Yamal and the attacking core played against Saudi Arabia less than five days ago and are unlikely to be fully rotated. Estadio Akron sits at approximately 5,100 feet altitude — a meaningful aerobic factor that suppresses second-half tempo and punishes pressing systems. Ronald Araújo is confirmed out for Uruguay; De Arrascaeta is also out, which guts their midfield creativity. Spain limited Saudi Arabia to 0.13 xG in a 4–0 win; Uruguay will need a completely different approach. Yamal's hamstring appears fully resolved and he is expected to start. The altitude and Uruguay's desperation make this less of a gimme than the -300 range Spain lines imply.

**New Zealand vs. Belgium — 11:00 PM ET | BC Place, Vancouver | Group G**
Belgium are fighting for second place against Iran in the parallel match. They have drawn both their group games (1–1 vs Egypt, 0–0 vs Iran) without a convincing attacking display. New Zealand are eliminated, but they drew Iran 2–2 in Matchday 1 — this is not a team that simply rolls over. Belgium's predicted lineup includes Verbruggen in goal, van de Ven and van Dijk in defence, with Malen and Depay going forward. Brobbey is questionable. BC Place is a dome — neutral controlled conditions. Belgium almost certainly need a win to lock second ahead of Iran on GD (both on 2pts going in), which means Tedesco cannot protect a lead; the shape has to stay open. That tactical pressure creates space for New Zealand on the counter — this is not a safe lay for Belgium.

**Egypt vs. Iran — 11:00 PM ET | Lumen Field, Seattle | Group G**
Egypt sit on top of Group G with 4pts and a win here clinches first. Iran are on 2pts and need a win to secure second outright; a draw keeps them alive depending on what Belgium do. Egypt's tournament form is the story: their 3–1 defeat of New Zealand was the first World Cup win in Egypt's history since their debut in 1934. Egypt play a compact mid-block and transition quickly through Mohamed Salah, who has been the most dangerous player in this group. Iran play a similar defensive structure and rely on counter-attacks; Sardar Azmoun is their main threat. The Pride Match branding controversy — both Egypt and Iran formally objected to FIFA about the Seattle local committee's designation — has dominated the pre-match narrative, but on the field this is a tightly matched group-decider. Two tight defensive teams with strong motivations to not concede; respect the total.

## Predictions

#### Norway vs. France

If Saliba is ruled out, France are a different defensive unit. In their two wins, Saliba was central to France's ability to set a high line and win aerial duels; Upamecano, while talented, has been exploited in one-on-one situations all season. Norway's entire forward structure is built around Haaland's direct runs in behind — exactly the scenario France's defense would be less equipped to handle without Saliba. France have been excellent in possession (Tchouaméni, Olise, Mbappé all at their best), but Norway's pressing system, which has generated 7 goals in two games, will force errors in the build-up phase. Norway have shown they can score against quality opposition and their defensive block has limited both Senegal and Iraq to few quality chances. Even with Saliba available, Norway's Haaland at 4 WC goals and 0.27 xG/shot leading all players in the tournament make this a genuine attacking matchup. Both teams are motivated to win top spot.

Norway are motivated to win and have the direct attacking profile to make this competitive regardless of Saliba's status. France will likely control possession but are vulnerable on the break; Norway will not park. Over 2.5 goals has covered in 7 straight France games and 11 consecutive matches overall. Predicted score: 2–1 (France).

#### Senegal vs. Iraq

Both teams have zero points and zero motivation — Senegal have been outclassed in both matches (conceded 4 goals), Iraq have shipped 7. Mane and Jackson will see this as an opportunity to build confidence; Ali Al Hamadi is Iraq's only consistent attacking threat. Senegal should dominate in any real sense but without Mendy in goal and with a scrambled backline, conceding a goal is realistic. Senegal are the stronger team and should win, but the margin will likely be modest.

Predicted score: 2–1 (Senegal).

#### Cape Verde vs. Saudi Arabia

Cape Verde play with genuine urgency and a defined tactical identity — they held Spain to a goalless draw through collective defensive organization and pressing in transition. Saudi Arabia, with nothing to play for, are likely to rotate and have shown no attacking coherence in their 4–0 dismantling against Spain. The Houston heat at 8 PM is reduced from peak but still a factor over 90 minutes; a Cape Verde side acclimatized to hot conditions may benefit slightly. The question is whether Cape Verde can break down a parked Saudi block — their 2-goals-in-2-games output is modest. A Cape Verde win is the most likely outcome but they may not cover a large spread.

Predicted score: 2–0 (Cape Verde).

#### Uruguay vs. Spain

Uruguay without Araújo and De Arrascaeta have defensive organization they can maintain through Giménez and Bentancur, but their attacking threat is severely blunted. Spain at 5,100 feet altitude have the possession and pressing quality to dominate despite the conditions, but if they rotate even four or five starters, the margin narrows. Valverde and Ugarte in the Uruguayan midfield will make this competitive for 60–70 minutes, but Spain's depth means even their rotation players (Oyarzabal, Cucurella) are quality. Uruguay's desperation means they won't sit deep — they'll try to press and win second balls, which plays into Spain's transition game. Spain should win, but altitude and Uruguay's defensive structure make a clean sheet harder than the lines imply.

Predicted score: 2–1 (Spain).

#### New Zealand vs. Belgium

Belgium's inability to score in two games is not just a statistical blip — their attack has looked pedestrian and disconnected. Malen on the right and Depay through the middle haven't found combinations; De Bruyne has been carrying an unconfirmed knock that has limited his movement. New Zealand play organized 4–4–2 defending and can threaten on set pieces, which is how they held Iran and gave teams fits. Belgium need a win, meaning they can't sit back — that open shape is precisely what gets them in trouble. New Zealand won't win this, but Belgium may not cover.

Predicted score: 2–1 (Belgium).

#### Egypt vs. Iran

Egypt's 3–1 win over New Zealand was built on Salah's creativity, a compact block, and clinical finishing. Iran play a near-identical structural setup — they'll sit in a mid-block and look to counter through Azmoun. The tactical similarity means this is less about one team dominating and more about which side can create the decisive moment. Egypt's set-piece delivery and Salah's movement in transition give them the marginal edge. Iran need to win, which means they'll need to commit men forward, exposing their back line to Salah-led counters in the second half.

Predicted score: 1–0 (Egypt).

## Around the Tournament

- Norway's return to the World Cup for the first time since 1998 has produced one of the tournament's most viral fan moments: the "Viking Row," where supporters sit shoulder to shoulder and row in unison to a drumbeat after each goal. After beating Senegal 3–2, the Norwegian players joined them in the stands.

- The Egypt vs. Iran match at Lumen Field has been formally designated a "Pride Match" by the local Seattle committee, prompting official objections from both the Egyptian Football Association and the Iranian Football Federation — both citing national laws criminalizing homosexuality. FIFA has not intervened. It's the most diplomatically loaded fixture of the group stage, being played tonight.

- South Africa's qualification for the knockout rounds is the first in their history, sealed Wednesday behind Thapelo Maseko's winner against South Korea. The vuvuzelas at Monterrey were reportedly audible three blocks from the stadium.

- Norwegian fans toured a Bass Pro Shops in one host city like a theme park, marveling at the hunting and fishing gear selections. A group of Scottish supporters in Boston belted out John Denver's "Take Me Home, Country Roads" in an American bar — a geographic misunderstanding that somehow made perfect sense.

- France midfielder Aurélien Tchouaméni confirmed that the French squad is "on a mission to make absent coach Didier Deschamps proud" — Deschamps is coaching his final World Cup and the players appear genuinely motivated by the send-off angle. Six goals scored, one conceded in two matches. The results back it up.
