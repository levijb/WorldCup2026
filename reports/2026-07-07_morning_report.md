## Today's Matches

<a name="argentina-vs-egypt"></a>
**Argentina vs. Egypt — 12:00 PM ET | Mercedes-Benz Stadium, Atlanta**
Messi has scored in every Argentina match this tournament and sits on 7 goals from an xG of just 3.82 — a finishing rate no matchup has slowed down yet, and Egypt's defense has allowed at least one goal in all four of its matches so far. Argentina needed extra time and a 111th-minute own goal to escape Cape Verde last time out, so there's a real (if distant) upset gene alive in this bracket, but Egypt's threat is almost entirely Salah, who has been quiet and carrying a hamstring niggle since the group stage. Egypt's back line is banged up — center-back questions have lingered since the Australia game — against a Messi-led attack that hasn't needed to press very hard to create chances all tournament. This is the widest talent gap left in the Round of 16, and the market (-290/+950) reflects it; the value is in exactly how thoroughly Argentina wins, not whether they do.

<a name="switzerland-vs-colombia"></a>
**Switzerland vs. Colombia — 4:00 PM ET | BC Place, Vancouver**
This is the tightest tie remaining in the round — Colombia have conceded once in four matches and generated 20-plus shots in three straight games, while Switzerland have quietly won three in a row behind Breel Embolo and breakout scorer Johan Manzambi. Colombia lost starting striker Jhon Cordoba to a tournament-ending hamstring injury in the last match, with Luis Suarez stepping in and immediately assisting the winner; Luis Diaz has 16 shots but only one goal and will be hunting his first real breakthrough of the knockout rounds. Switzerland have conceded in 8 of their last 10 matches overall despite the clean scoresheets in this tournament, which is the one crack in an otherwise well-organized side. Both teams are healthy up and down the rest of the squad, and neither side wants to open the game up first — expect a slim margin decided by a single moment, with real ET/PK risk.

<a name="predictions"></a>
## Predictions

<a name="predictions-argentina-vs-egypt"></a>
#### Argentina vs. Egypt
Argentina have been the most clinical side left in the bracket even when the underlying performance wobbled, and Messi's 7 goals from 3.82 xG is the standout individual stat of the entire tournament — he is materially outperforming a very good expected-goals rate, not just riding one. Egypt reached the knockout rounds for the first time in their history by beating Australia on penalties, but their route here has been through resilience and set pieces rather than control, and every one of their four matches has ended with at least one goal conceded. Emam Ashour is Egypt's leading scorer with two, and their best path to a chance runs through Salah dropping deep to link play rather than anything resembling sustained pressure — the same profile of threat that gave Argentina some trouble against Cape Verde, but a level below what Cape Verde actually produced.

Argentina should control possession and territory throughout, and the question is margin rather than result — Egypt's best realistic outcome is staying compact, absorbing pressure, and hoping for a set piece or a defensive mistake to keep it tense into the second half. A repeat of the Cape Verde scare is possible given Argentina's defense has shown real lapses this tournament, but Egypt lack the attacking depth Cape Verde had to actually capitalize on those lapses. Predicted score: 2–0 (Argentina).

<a name="predictions-switzerland-vs-colombia"></a>
#### Switzerland vs. Colombia
Colombia's defensive numbers are the best story in this match — three clean sheets in four matches, only 2.7 xG allowed all tournament — but they're now without Cordoba up top, and Luis Suarez taking over central duties changes the shape of their attack from a runner to more of a target man who links rather than stretches defenses himself. Switzerland's front three of Embolo, Manzambi, and Ndoye have combined for real output (Manzambi alone has 3 goals and 2 assists, the youngest player to reach 5 goal contributions at a World Cup since 1966), and Switzerland have scored in every match this tournament despite conceding regularly. Both midfields are well-organized enough that individual quality — Luis Diaz getting isolated wide, or Manzambi finding pockets between lines — is more likely to decide this than a sustained tactical breakdown.

This has the profile of a match that stays level deep into the second half: neither side wants to commit numbers forward first, and both have shown they can grind out a tight one-goal result. Colombia's attacking talent gives them a path to a goal even in a match Switzerland controls for long stretches, but Switzerland's habit of conceding regularly means this doesn't have to stay 0-0 for either side to feel good about their chances. Extra time is a live outcome given how cagey both sides have been in big moments this tournament. Predicted score: 1–1 (Draw — goes to ET/PKs, Colombia advances).

<a name="around-the-tournament"></a>
## Around the Tournament

- Cristiano Ronaldo's international career ended in tears at AT&T Stadium. "I'm leaving with a clear conscience," he said afterward, adding that he'd given his all and was sad to see it end this way. He becomes the first player to score in six different World Cups, a record he shares with no one, and finishes with 11 World Cup goals across 27 appearances — the second-most appearances in tournament history, behind only Messi's 30.
- All three World Cup co-hosts are out in the same round for the first time in a multi-host tournament — Canada, Mexico, and the USA all eliminated in the Round of 16. Mexico's Guillermo Ochoa and Germany's Manuel Neuer also played their likely final World Cups this week, alongside Ronaldo, in what's shaping up as a genuine changing-of-the-guard tournament.
- Belgium's Charles De Ketelaere scored twice in the first half against the USA, and manager Rudi Garcia sprang a surprise by benching both Kevin De Bruyne and Jeremy Doku — a gamble that paid off immediately.
- Pochettino refused to blame the Balogun controversy for the USA's exit: "We don't need to find excuses. I think we were not good enough." Balogun himself called the week an "emotional roller coaster" but said he tried to be a positive presence on the pitch regardless.
- Spain has now gone six straight matches without conceding, a World Cup record, and Ronaldo — of all people — was the only Portugal player to register a shot on target in the match that ended his career.
