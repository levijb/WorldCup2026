## Tournament Status

- **Day 26 — Round of 16, day 3 of 4.** Two matches today; two more tomorrow close out the round.
- **12 teams remain.** Four are already through to the quarterfinals — Morocco, France, Norway, and England — with the other eight playing off across today and tomorrow for the final four QF spots.
- **Two of the four quarterfinals are already set:** France vs. Morocco, July 9 in Foxborough, and Norway vs. England, July 11 at Hard Rock Stadium in Miami. Today's and tomorrow's winners will fill out the remaining two QF pairings.
- **Brazil and Mexico are out.** Norway eliminated Brazil 2-1 yesterday — the five-time champions' earliest exit since 1990. England beat co-host Mexico 3-2, its first-ever win at the Azteca.
- **5 of the pre-tournament top-8 Opta favorites remain alive** (Spain, France, England, Argentina, Portugal). Brazil joins Germany and Netherlands among the eliminated.

## Today's Matches

<a name="portugal-vs-spain"></a>
**Portugal vs. Spain — 3:00 PM ET | AT&T Stadium, Arlington, Texas**
Spain haven't conceded a goal in four matches and arrive unbeaten in 34 straight games; Portugal have needed late drama twice just to get here, including a 94th-minute Goncalo Ramos winner over Croatia after Ronaldo's penalty — his first-ever World Cup knockout goal in his sixth tournament. Mikel Oyarzabal has four goals and is Spain's whole attacking engine when Yamal isn't cooking; Portugal's central pairing of Joao Neves and Vitinha against Spain's Rodri and Pedri is the game's real battle. Nico Williams is out for Spain with a hamstring injury, otherwise both squads are healthy. This is a rivalry match between genuine peers — the market's -115/+310 pricing is fair, and the real value sits in the prop market rather than picking a side outright.

<a name="usa-vs-belgium"></a>
**USA vs. Belgium — 8:00 PM ET | Lumen Field, Seattle**
The USA's leading scorer Folarin Balogun is playing today only because FIFA suspended his automatic one-match ban after President Trump personally called FIFA president Gianni Infantino — the first time since 1962 a red card hasn't produced a suspension, and Belgium is furious about it. Strip away the controversy and this is a genuine coin-flip: the USA have won every match with their first-choice XI, Belgium has needed a late comeback (Senegal) and an unconvincing group stage to get here, and both defenses have leaked chances all tournament. Pulisic is the USA's primary creator and penalty taker; Charles De Ketelaere leads Belgium's line with Lukaku as an impact sub rather than a starter. Sea-level, no weather concerns, and a home crowd that's carried the USA deep into this tournament — the game's own edge over the noise around it is the better story, and the props reflect that.

## Predictions

<a name="predictions-portugal-vs-spain"></a>
#### Portugal vs. Spain
Spain's defensive record this tournament (zero goals conceded in four matches) is the single most important fact in this match — Rodri anchors a back four that has faced nothing it couldn't handle, and Portugal will need genuine individual moments rather than sustained pressure to break it down. Portugal's own path here has been unconvincing by results if not by underlying quality: they were held by DR Congo and Colombia in the group stage, and it took a 94th-minute Goncalo Ramos winner to beat Croatia after Ronaldo converted his first-ever World Cup knockout goal from the penalty spot. Oyarzabal has four goals from just two matches — braces against both Saudi Arabia and Austria — and remains Spain's most reliable route to goal even with Yamal drawing most of the attention; Portugal's route back is Bruno Fernandes finding pockets between Spain's lines or Ronaldo drawing another penalty, since open-play chances against this defense have been scarce for everyone.

Spain's control of midfield through Rodri and Pedri should let them dictate tempo for long stretches, but Portugal have shown in this tournament that they can be dangerous exactly when a game looks settled — both of their knockout-stage goals so far have come from moments manufactured rather than built. Given the head-to-head history between these sides is close to even (Portugal beat Spain on penalties in last year's Nations League final) and neither side has faced a test quite like this yet, a one-goal margin either way or extra time are all live outcomes. Predicted score: 2–1 (Spain).

<a name="predictions-usa-vs-belgium"></a>
#### USA vs. Belgium
Set aside the Balogun saga and the underlying matchup is close to even — the USA have been undefeated with their strongest lineup on the field, while Belgium has needed a stoppage-time-into-extra-time escape against Senegal and unconvincing draws with Egypt and Iran to reach this point. Balogun's return restores the exact system Pochettino built the attack around: his hold-up play and pressing opens space for Pulisic to operate as the creative hub, rather than forcing Ricardo Pepi into an unfamiliar lone-striker role. Belgium's attack runs through Kevin De Bruyne and Charles De Ketelaere with Romelu Lukaku held in reserve as an impact substitute — a sign Belgium's coaching staff doesn't fully trust his match fitness yet, even though he's the shortest-priced scorer on the board. Both defenses have given up real chances all tournament, which points toward a match that stays open rather than one either side controls fully.

The psychological subplot here is real: Belgium's federation views the reversal itself as the injustice, and every player in that dressing room knows it, which cuts both ways — it could sharpen Belgium's focus or simply add pressure to a team that's already looked shaky at times. A raucous, pro-USA crowd in Seattle and the emotional lift of getting their top scorer back give the hosts a genuine edge, but Belgium has the more experienced core in a knockout moment like this. Expect goals at both ends and very little cushion either way. Predicted score: 2–2 (Draw — goes to ET/PKs, USA advances).

<a name="around-the-tournament"></a>
## Around the Tournament

- President Trump called FIFA president Gianni Infantino last week to ask about Balogun's red card; FIFA's Disciplinary Committee then suspended the automatic ban under Article 27 of its own code — the first time a World Cup red card hasn't produced a suspension since 1962, when Brazil's Garrincha was cleared to play the final. Belgium's federation said it was "astonished" by the reversal and has been granted the right to formally appeal; UEFA also criticized the decision. Trump posted on Truth Social thanking FIFA "for doing what was right."
- Mexican fans surrounded England's team hotel overnight with horns, speakers, and fireworks trying to disrupt their sleep ahead of the match — England reportedly slept fine anyway. The match itself was then delayed an hour by a lightning storm that sent fans and staff scrambling for cover under the Azteca's roof.
- Jude Bellingham called it the biggest England win he can remember "as a fan or player." The result also buries a 40-year ghost at the Azteca, the site of Maradona's Hand of God in 1986.
- Erling Haaland, after eliminating Brazil: "I never expected to win against Brazil." He admitted he'd doubted it was even possible, and his Norway teammates joked afterward that they're just glad he plays for them.
- England's Jordan Henderson was taken to hospital after the Mexico win — not from anything on the pitch, but from tumbling over an advertising board during the full-time celebrations.
