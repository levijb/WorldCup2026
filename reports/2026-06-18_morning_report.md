Both teams arrive here having lost their openers and knowing a second defeat almost certainly ends their tournament. **Switzerland vs. Bosnia & Herzegovina | 3:00 PM ET | SoFi Stadium, Los Angeles | Group B**

Both teams drew 1–1 in their openers — Switzerland conceded a 93rd-minute equalizer to Qatar in one of the more improbable finishes of the early rounds, while Bosnia held Canada. Switzerland's xG in that match was elite; their 26 shots were the most they've managed in a World Cup game. The Swiss should have more than a point to show for game one. Bosnia captain Kolašinac limped off late against Canada and is a late fitness test; his absence would weaken an already outgunned defensive structure. Switzerland are -180, which is a reasonable price for a side that dominated their opener and face a depleted opponent — the draw at +310 is the market's way of telling you it isn't impossible, but this setup favors Switzerland getting the job done.

**Canada vs. Qatar | 6:00 PM ET | BC Place, Vancouver | Group B**

The return of Alphonso Davies changes this match materially. Canada's opener without him was functional but muted; Davies' presence alters the entire left channel and the team's capacity to transition at pace. Jesse Marsch confirmed Davies is available, though his minutes may be managed — even 60 minutes of Davies is a different proposition than what Qatar will have faced on film. Qatar opened with a 1–1 draw against Switzerland and showed genuine organization, but they are being asked to back that up away from home in front of what will be a raucous Vancouver crowd. Canada are -320, reflecting their structural advantage, though the line has drifted slightly from -360, which is marginally interesting. The angle here is less the moneyline and more what Qatar do with the ball in a disciplined defensive shape.

**Mexico vs. South Korea | 9:00 PM ET | Estadio Akron, Guadalajara | Group A**

This is the marquee match of the day and the most difficult to price. Mexico come in off a 2–0 win over South Africa and play at altitude in Guadalajara — roughly 5,100 feet — where the aerobic demands on a visiting side are genuinely significant.
