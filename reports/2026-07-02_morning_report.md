<a name="todays-matches"></a>
## Today's Matches

**Spain vs. Austria — 3:00 PM ET | SoFi Stadium, Inglewood, California**
Spain arrive as -300 favorites with a defense that hasn't conceded in three games, but the attack that couldn't beat Cape Verde or Uruguay in the group stage now does it without both starting wide options — Nico Williams (groin) and Yeremy Pino (shoulder) are out, both hurt in rough Uruguay-game challenges. Lamine Yamal's minutes have climbed every match (19, 45, 76) as he builds back from the hamstring tear that shadowed his spring, but his output has been quiet since the Saudi Arabia goal — one shot, three crosses in 76 minutes against Uruguay. Austria needed a 96th-minute Sasa Kalajdzic equalizer just to escape Group J in third, and both David Alaba and Marko Arnautovic picked up knocks in that game, though Ralf Rangnick expects both available. Spain haven't actually won a World Cup knockout match since Iniesta's 2010 final winner — group-stage exit in 2014, penalty losses in 2018 and 2022 — a real data point for a team riding a 34-match unbeaten run into a game it should still win. The gap between "Spain wins" and "Spain wins comfortably" is wider than -300 suggests.

**Portugal vs. Croatia — 7:00 PM ET | BMO Field, Toronto**
Cristiano Ronaldo, presumably in his sixth and final World Cup, faces Luka Modrić's Croatia in a matchup that already turned historic when Ronaldo's brace against Uzbekistan made him the first player to score in six different World Cups. Portugal's group form undersold their talent — a 5-0 statement over Uzbekistan bookended by a 1-1 draw with Congo DR and a 0-0 with Colombia that dropped their possession to 45%, their lowest share since last year's Nations League final — while Croatia recovered from an opening 4-2 mauling by England to grind out wins over Panama and Ghana. Roberto Martínez has a full squad available, with João Neves back alongside Vitinha and Bruno Fernandes in midfield against a Croatian back four that conceded four goals in 90 minutes three weeks ago. Zlatko Dalić has scrapped the back three that failed against England for a flat four, betting that structure solves what pace exposed in the opener. Portugal's individual quality says one thing; a team that's drawn two of three and struggled to break down a packed defense says another.

**Switzerland vs. Algeria — 11:00 PM ET | BC Place, Vancouver**
Vladimir Petković spent seven years building this Swiss program before Murat Yakin inherited it in 2021 — now he's on the opposite bench trying to take it down with Algeria, in a matchup between a team that hasn't lost since the tournament began and one that's conceded seven goals in three group games. Switzerland topped Group B unbeaten, outscoring opponents 7-3, with Young Player Award contender Johan Manzambi contributing three goals and an assist in three appearances, only one of them a start. Algeria stumbled through as a third-place qualifier — a 3-0 opening loss to Argentina, a 2-1 recovery over Jordan, and a wild 3-3 draw with Austria that could have gone either way in stoppage time — and striker Mohammed Amoura is now a fitness doubt, leaving Amine Gouiri to lead the line. Riyad Mahrez scored twice in that Austria game and remains, at 35, Algeria's only reliably dangerous outlet. Switzerland have no injury concerns and should control the game through the Xhaka-Freuler platform that's carried them all tournament — the question is whether Algeria's defense, the leakiest of any team left in the bracket, holds up any better than it has all June.

## Predictions

#### Spain vs. Austria
Spain's defensive record travels intact into the knockout rounds, but their attacking identity is compromised with Williams and Pino both out, pushing Alex Baena into a wide role he's filled in flashes rather than for 90 minutes against a set defense. Austria's counter is built around moments rather than territory — they'll cede the ball for long stretches and lean on Sabitzer, Laimer, and a front-footed Arnautovic (fitness permitting) to break at pace, the same shape that let them come back from the brink against Algeria. Yamal is the swing factor: his minutes have climbed every match, and if he plays close to 90 against a back line that's conceded three to Algeria alone, Spain's ceiling rises fast. Without their natural width fully healthy, though, this looks more like a team that needs a set piece or an individual moment than one that carves teams open in the run of play.
Expect Spain to control the ball without ever fully running away with it, with the breakthrough coming from a set piece or a second-half Yamal moment rather than sustained pressure. Predicted score: 2–0 (Spain).

#### Portugal vs. Croatia
Portugal's talent gap over Croatia is real — four PSG players, a front line of Ronaldo, Rafael Leão, Pedro Neto and João Félix — but the group stage never found rhythm, and Croatia's specific weakness (four conceded to England's width) is exactly the kind of thing Portugal's wide options are built to exploit. The midfield battle between João Neves and Vitinha's press-resistance and 300-plus combined caps of Modrić and Kovačić will likely decide how much service Portugal's attack actually sees, since Croatia's plan is to sit in and make Portugal solve them in the final third rather than in transition. Ronaldo has two goals this tournament and remains first in line for service, with Bruno Fernandes' set-piece deliveries the likeliest secondary route through a well-organized but unspectacular Croatian back four. Dalić's switch to a flat four should hold up better than the back three did against England, but it hasn't been tested yet against a front line this deep.
This has the shape of a game that stays tight into the final half hour before Portugal's individual class finds a way through — Croatia can hold a structure, but not against this much sustained quality in the final third. Predicted score: 2–0 (Portugal).

#### Switzerland vs. Algeria
Switzerland's platform is control: Xhaka and Freuler screening a back line that's conceded three goals in three games, freeing Ruben Vargas and Dan Ndoye to attack the width Algeria has to defend without its first-choice striker. Algeria have scored in every match this tournament, though, and Mahrez's brace against Austria is a reminder that one moment from their captain can flip a scoreline Switzerland's structure otherwise controls — the issue for Petković's side isn't creating chances, it's the seven goals they've already conceded doing the same thing to opponents that Switzerland now gets to attack. Manzambi gives Yakin a release valve capable of beating a man in a midfield battle Algeria hasn't consistently won this tournament, and if the Swiss double pivot dominates the middle third the way it has all group stage, service into the final third should stay steady even without a true first-choice striker. This is a more complete team against a more talented individual attacker, and single-elimination football tends to favor the side that doesn't need a moment of magic to win.
Expect Switzerland to control territory and draw at least one defensive mistake, with Mahrez's quality keeping this from ever feeling fully safe. Predicted score: 2–1 (Switzerland).

## Around the Tournament

- Cape Verde's 40-year-old goalkeeper Vozinha has become the story of the tournament — seven saves and a clean sheet in a scoreless draw with Spain that denied Lamine Yamal, and an Instagram following that's jumped from 500,000 to 17.4 million ahead of Friday's date with Argentina.
- Kylian Mbappé's brace against Sweden made him the all-time leading scorer in World Cup knockout-stage history, while Didier Deschamps set the record for most World Cup wins as a manager (18) — a milestone reached during a stretch where he flew home for his mother's funeral and missed a match in absentia.
- Mexico's 2-0 win over Ecuador ended a 40-year World Cup knockout curse in front of 114,000 fans at the Azteca; Raúl Jiménez's goal also made him Mexico's second all-time leading scorer.
- Folarin Balogun scored and was sent off in the same match against Bosnia and Herzegovina — the first player from any country to do both in a knockout game since Zidane in the 2006 final — and will now miss the USA's round-of-16 date with Belgium.
- Erling Haaland scored the winner as Norway won their first-ever World Cup knockout match; the goal extended a run of 25 goals in his last 13 competitive Norway appearances and sets up a heavyweight round-of-16 clash with Brazil.
